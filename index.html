Jordan, characterized by its arid to semi-arid climate, faces unique challenges in ensuring **food security** for its growing population. The nation's resilience is built on optimizing scarce water resources and adopting advanced agricultural technologies. This page explores the key components of food production in the Hashemite Kingdom.

Around 80% of the land is arid or semi-arid, making water a critical resource. Wheat, barley, and olives are the most common rain-fed crops, while vegetables and citrus dominate irrigated farms.

Irrigation systems

Modern irrigation in Jordan includes **drip irrigation**, **sprinkler systems**, and limited surface irrigation. Drip irrigation is the most widespread as it saves water and improves efficiency. Innovative methods such as water harvesting and treated wastewater reuse are increasingly applied to face water scarcity.

Available or rare crops

Common crops in Jordan include **tomatoes**, **cucumbers**, **eggplants**, **olives**, and **citrus fruits**. Rare or less available crops include tropical fruits like mangoes, pineapples, and avocados due to climate challenges. Some pilot projects are testing new varieties to expand food production.

Available or rare plants

Jordan hosts a wide range of **medicinal and aromatic plants** such as sage, thyme, and rosemary. Rare native species include some desert plants and wildflowers that are endangered due to overgrazing and climate change. Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect biodiversity.

Vertical farming

**Vertical farming** is an emerging technology in Jordan. It involves growing crops in stacked layers within controlled environments. This method reduces water use by up to 90%, requires less land, and allows year-round production, making it a promising solution for future food security.
